Australia’s Tourism Recovery Dashboard

Exploring how international visitors and spending trends evolved across Australian states (2022–2025).

Introduction

This dashboard visualises the recovery of Australia’s tourism industry following the global travel slowdown. It combines international arrival trends, spending distribution across states, and economic correlations to reveal how tourism has shaped Australia’s post-pandemic growth story.

Together, these charts show how visitor volumes, expenditure, and geography interact to form a complete picture of Australia’s tourism resurgence from 2022 to 2025.

International Visitor Arrivals by Top 5 Source Countries

This line chart tracks tourism recovery among Australia’s top source countries. Observe how nations differ in pace and magnitude of rebound.

New Zealand and China lead the rebound, reflecting early travel corridor agreements and restored air connectivity.
Meanwhile, arrivals from UK and the US show steady post-2023 acceleration followed by India.

Where Do Tourists Spend the Most?

Each circle represents a state. Circle size indicates total international tourist expenditure in Billions (AUD), highlighting how economic activity concentrates geographically.

New South Wales and Victoria dominate international tourism spending due to their urban attractions and global events, while Queensland continues its rapid growth powered by leisure destinations like the Gold Coast and Cairns.

Visitors vs Expenditure by State

This scatter plot compares visitor numbers with tourism expenditure per state, across multiple years. Use the dropdown to explore annual shifts in performance.

A strong positive correlation shows that higher visitor counts directly translate into greater spending.
New South Wales and Victoria consistently lead in both visitors and revenue, while Queensland’s upward trajectory signals growing international interest by 2025.

Key Takeaways

  • Australia’s tourism fully rebounded by 2025, with arrivals exceeding pre-pandemic levels for top markets.
  • NSW, VIC, and QLD together account for over 75% of total international expenditure.
  • Leisure-focused destinations outpaced business travel in recovery, reflecting shifting global tourism behaviour.
  • The positive correlation between visitor counts and expenditure underscores tourism’s central role in Australia’s service economy.

Data Sources: Tourism Research Australia, Australian Bureau of Statistics (2022–2025)
